# Tips & Tricks

A few handy things to know that will make PawLog even more useful.

---

## Swipe Left to Skip Without Breaking Your Streak

If today's just not happening — bad weather, Rigby is under the weather, or you're away — swipe left on any task in the **Routine** tab to excuse it with a reason. Your streak is protected and the excused day is logged for the record.

---

## Set Up Holiday Mode Before You Go Away

Before leaving for a trip, set up **Holiday Mode** in **More → Notification Settings** with your travel dates. PawLog will automatically excuse all tasks for those days and silence notifications while you're gone. You won't come back to broken streaks!

See [[Holiday Mode]] for full details.

---

## Multi-Pet Walks Save Time

If your household has two dogs and they always walk together, use the **"Who's coming?"** selector when you start a walk session. Both pets get their walk logged at once — one tap, two records.

See [[Walk Tracking]] for full details.

---

## Use the Emergency Info Card as a Pet Sitter Handoff

Before leaving your pet with a sitter, open the **Emergency Info Card** from the **Emergency** button on the home screen. Show it to them (or screenshot it) — it has everything they need: breed, age, medications, and key notes, all in one easy-to-read view.

See [[Emergency Features]] for full details.

---

## Keep Your Emergency Contacts Updated

Add your regular vet, your nearest 24-hour emergency animal hospital, and a trusted friend or pet sitter to **Emergency Contacts**. In a crisis you'll be able to call or navigate with one tap — no fumbling through your phone's contacts.

---

## Set a Realistic Daily Reminder Time

In **Notification Settings**, set your daily reminder for a time when you're usually home and have a moment to check in — not first thing in the morning when you're rushing, but maybe late afternoon. This way the reminder is actually useful rather than something you immediately swipe away.

---

## Walk History Is Surprisingly Motivating

Check the **History → Walks** tab after a week of regular walks. Seeing your walk history grouped by day with actual durations is a great reminder of the effort you're putting in. You might find yourself going for that extra evening walk just to add another entry!

## Back Up Regularly

Export a backup after any big changes — adding a new pet, recording a vet visit, or updating medications. Copy it to cloud storage (Google Drive, OneDrive, etc.) so it's safe even if your phone is lost or reset.

See [[Backup and Restore]] for full details.
